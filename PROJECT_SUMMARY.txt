================================================================================
                          AQI CATEGORIES EXPLAINED
================================================================================

AQI 1 - Good (Green)
- Air quality is satisfactory
- Air pollution poses little or no risk

AQI 2 - Moderate (Yellow)
- Air quality is acceptable
- Some pollutants may be a concern for sensitive people

AQI 3 - Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (Orange)
- Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects
- General public is less likely to be affected

AQI 4 - Unhealthy (Red)
- Everyone may begin to experience health effects
- Sensitive groups may experience more serious effects

AQI 5 - Very Unhealthy (Purple)
- Health alert: everyone may experience serious health effects
- Outdoor activities should be avoided

================================================================================
                        POLLUTANTS MONITORED
================================================================================

1. PM2.5 - Fine Particulate Matter (< 2.5 micrometers)
   - Most harmful to human health
   - Primary AQI indicator

2. PM10 - Coarse Particulate Matter (< 10 micrometers)
   - Affects respiratory system

3. CO - Carbon Monoxide
   - Reduces oxygen delivery to organs

4. NO2 - Nitrogen Dioxide
   - Causes respiratory problems

5. O3 - Ozone
   - Ground-level ozone, harmful to lungs

6. SO2 - Sulfur Dioxide
   - Causes respiratory issues

All measurements in µg/m³ (micrograms per cubic meter)
